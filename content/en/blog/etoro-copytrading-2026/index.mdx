CopyTrading is often marketed as the easy shortcut into investing. In practice, it is better understood as a **learning and execution tool**, not a performance guarantee.

## How it works

According to eToro's official CopyTrader pages, you choose a public investor, allocate an amount, and then your portfolio mirrors that investor's positions in real time. You can:

- start or stop copying,
- add or remove funds,
- pause the copy,
- define a stop loss for the copy relationship.

The key point is that you are copying **a process and a risk profile**, not just a historical return figure.

## Minimums and limits

eToro's official resources currently state:

- a **$200 minimum** to start copying one investor,
- a **$1 minimum** for each copied position,
- up to **100 investors** copied simultaneously,
- no **extra fee** for using CopyTrader itself.

That does not remove ordinary trading frictions. Spreads, transaction costs, FX conversion, or product-level fees can still affect your results.

## How to pick investors to copy

The most useful filters are not the most exciting ones. Review:

1. consistency across multiple periods,
2. the displayed risk score,
3. portfolio composition across stocks, ETFs, crypto, and CFDs,
4. trading frequency,
5. diversification logic.

A trader with strong recent returns but heavy concentration, leverage, or crypto exposure may be a poor fit for a beginner.

## Common mistakes

- Copying based only on short-term performance.
- Copying several investors without checking overlapping exposures.
- Ignoring currency conversion, spreads, and your own allocation size.
- Treating CopyTrader as an autopilot system.

## Checklist before you copy

- Confirm the **$200 minimum** and your true budget.
- Review the investor's main holdings and turnover.
- Check whether CFDs, leverage, or crypto dominate the strategy.
- Define the maximum loss you are willing to tolerate.
- Start small and monitor behavior over time before scaling.

CopyTrading can speed up learning, but it still has to be managed like a real investment decision: with limits, monitoring, and a clear exit plan.
