“Fees” is often too broad a label. On Bitpanda, it is more useful to think in terms of **total execution cost**, because the final number depends on the service and the order itself.

## What Bitpanda says

Bitpanda's help article about fees and premiums explains that costs depend on the service used. That means the smart check is the **final order preview**, not a generic assumption carried from one product to another.

## Crypto

For crypto buys and sells, Bitpanda explains that **premiums** and/or fees may apply depending on the service. In practice:

- the exact cost needs to be checked at confirmation,
- order size matters,
- payment currency matters,
- part of the cost can already be reflected in the displayed price.

## Real Stocks & ETFs

Bitpanda's Real Stocks & ETFs help content is more specific:

- trades are executed on a **regulated stock exchange**,
- the user holds **real ownership**,
- the current help content states a **fixed €1 fee per trade**.

That is useful, but it still does not replace checking the full order preview before execution.

## Minimum size and order logic

The practical issue is not only “how much does it cost?”, but also **how big is the trade?** On very small tickets, a fixed fee or visible premium becomes heavier in percentage terms.

Before buying, check:

1. which currency you are using,
2. whether the order is large enough to absorb a fixed fee or premium,
3. whether the purchase is meant for short-term trading or longer accumulation.

## Checklist before confirmation

- Read the total cost on the final ticket.
- Confirm whether you are buying crypto or Real Stocks & ETFs.
- Check the payment currency.
- Avoid stacking many tiny orders if the fixed cost becomes too heavy.
- Compare the total visible cost before you click confirm.

The right approach is not to look for a magic generic fee, but to review **the final displayed cost for your actual order**.
