<p class="eyebrow">Update • March 17, 2026</p>

<p class="lede">Goal: stop staring at your eToro positions all day. These TradingView alerts help you monitor stocks, indices, ETFs, and cryptoassets when they are available in your region. They improve discipline, but they do not remove the need to review a setup before acting.</p>

<section id="prerequisites">
<h2>Quick prerequisites</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>The right TradingView plan:</strong> if you run several live scenarios, the pricing page currently lists 20 alerts on Essential, 100 on Plus, 400 on Premium, and 1,000 on Ultimate.</li>
<li><strong>Correct session settings:</strong> confirm the asset's market hours and timezone so you do not trigger alerts off-session.</li>
<li><strong>2FA for webhooks:</strong> TradingView requires two-factor authentication before webhook alerts can be created.</li>
<li><strong>Defined risk first:</strong> position size, invalidation, and exit logic should be decided before you send an order to eToro.</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section id="templates">
<h2>Alert templates to clone</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Breakout + volume:</strong> alert when price breaks resistance and volume is at least 20% above its recent average.</li>
<li><strong>Clean pullback:</strong> after a breakout, wait for price to revisit EMA20 or a short consolidation zone before re-evaluating entry.</li>
<li><strong>Defensive trailing stop:</strong> trigger an alert when price breaks the latest 1H low so you can reassess stop placement.</li>
<li><strong>Filtered crypto setup:</strong> when the asset is available on eToro, combine a VWAP cross with an RSI that still supports the uptrend.</li>
</ul>
<p>We cover more examples and screenshots in <a href="https://tradingview-avis.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">our full TradingView guide</a>. The template matters less than the discipline around how you adapt it.</p>
</section>

<section id="best-practices">
<h2>Best practices with eToro</h2>
<ul>
<li>Only create alerts on instruments that are actually available in your account and region.</li>
<li>If you are new, do not let alerts push you into leveraged CFD trades you do not fully understand.</li>
<li>Re-check spread, volatility, invalidation level, and position size before every order.</li>
<li>An alert is a review trigger, not a fully automated trading decision.</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section id="checklist">
<h2>Setup checklist</h2>
<ol>
<li>Create an “eToro” watchlist for your priority instruments.</li>
<li>Duplicate one breakout template and one pullback template across the markets you monitor weekly.</li>
<li>Write explicit alert messages with the asset, level, direction, and action to review.</li>
<li>Run a weekly cleanup to remove expired alerts and tighten stale levels.</li>
</ol>
</section>
